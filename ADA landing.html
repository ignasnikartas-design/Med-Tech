Why ADA

Most clinical AI guesses what to read. ADA asks.

Auto-summarising tools dump every word from the visit into a black box and hope the draft comes out right. When it doesn't, the doctor can't tell why — was it the transcript, an old note, a hallucinated detail?

ADA inverts the model. You curate the sources — transcript, typed observations, prior records — toggle what feeds each draft, and see the count of active sources at all times. The AI proposes. You approve, edit, or discard. Edited fields are never silently overwritten on regenerate.

“The first AI tool I've used where I always know what it just read. Regenerating a section doesn't wipe my edits — that one detail is why it's in my workflow.”
— GP, Affidea Vilnius (pilot, March 2026)

Capabilities

Four surfaces. One quiet workflow.

Built for the doctor's actual day — not for a demo. Each surface earns its place because the alternative is a slower visit.

Live scribe, in person or by call
One button starts capture — in the room or on a phone call to the patient. Lines stream live into the Transcript tab; click any line to correct STT output before it feeds a draft.
Context curation
A single drawer holds every source ADA can read: transcripts, typed observations, prior visits. Toggle what's in scope. A persistent count badge keeps the answer visible without opening anything.
Drafts you can trust
Anamnesis, differentials, referrals — drafted from the curated sources, structured to the local note format (E025 today). Every AI-generated field is badged. Edits are tracked field-by-field and never silently overwritten on regenerate.
Push to the EHR, one click
When the draft is ready, push it as a structured E025 record. Missing required sections surface as a danger pill before submit — nothing leaves ADA half-finished.
How it works

Three moves. One visit.

From the patient walking in to the note landing in the EHR.

1
Capture
Start the scribe at the visit or on the call. Type observations as you go. Both feed the Sources drawer automatically.
2
Curate & draft
Toggle which sources ADA reads. Ask for an anamnesis, differentials, or a referral. Edit any field — ADA tracks what's yours and what's drafted.
3
Push
Send the finished record to the EHR in one click, formatted as an E025 consultation note. Done before the next patient sits down.
